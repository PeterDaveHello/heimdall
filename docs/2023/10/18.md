## [Free IRS-run tax filing pilot to be available in 13 states](https://www.irs.gov/newsroom/irs-advances-innovative-direct-file-project-for-2024-tax-season-free-irs-run-pilot-option-projected-to-be-available-for-eligible-taxpayers-in-13-states)

- The IRS has unveiled a Direct File pilot program for the 2024 tax season, enabling eligible taxpayers in certain states to submit their federal tax returns directly and for free.
- Participating states include Arizona, California, Massachusetts, and New York. The Direct File service will be mobile-friendly, and structured as interview-based, covering specified income types and tax credits.
- The pilot's purpose is to evaluate the Direct File program's feasibility and efficiency in gathering crucial data for its further development. The IRS anticipates more refined details and changes before official launch.

### [Reactions](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=37920633)

- The Internal Revenue Service (IRS) has initiated a pilot program named "Direct File" in 13 states to offer free tax filing for certain taxpayers with simpler returns, typically lower-income individuals.
- The program is sparking discussions about the complicated tax filing system in the US, the roles of private tax filing companies, and the ongoing demand for a more straightforward tax code.
- The IRS's intervention by providing free online tax services has triggered a dialogue about the effects on private tax filing companies and the necessity of government involvement in simplifying tax processes.

## [Making CRDTs More Efficient](https://jakelazaroff.com/words/making-crdts-98-percent-more-efficient/)

- The blog post delves into strategies for enhancing the efficiency of Collaborative Real-Time Editors (CRDTs), specifically for a pixel art editor using techniques like hex codes, storing UUIDs (Universal Unique Identifiers) and colors in tables, and applying run-length encoding.
- Besides, it elaborates on data compression approaches like run-length and binary encoding to lessen the data size and discusses unique ways to compress CRDT state data through diverse optimization techniques leading to an impressive 98% reduction in size.
- It further mentions about CRDT libraries that have integrated these optimization techniques.

### [Reactions](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=37915934)

- The article suggests enhancing Conflict-free Replicated Data Types (CRDTs) efficiency through compression schemes and alternative methods for generating UUIDs (Universally Unique Identifiers).
- It discusses converting JSON-based data structures to binary format, further compressing them using algorithms like brotli and LZ4, and the potential of using LZ4 compression for over the wire data transfer.
- The possibility of exploiting lossless image compression algorithms like PNG in CRDTs was also explored, while performance issues with demos were discussed for improvement.

## [4K4D: Real-Time 4D View Synthesis at 4K Resolution](https://zju3dv.github.io/4k4d/)

- The paper introduces a method named 4K4D dedicated to real-time synthesis of dynamic 3D scenes at 4K resolution.
- It presents a 4D point cloud representation, which supports hardware rasterization leading to swift rendering.
- A new hybrid appearance model and a unique differentiable depth peeling algorithm are developed to boost the quality and learn the model from RGB videos. This outdoes the rendering speed and quality of previous methods.

### [Reactions](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=37923801)

- Researchers have developed a real-time 4D view synthesis system capable of rendering 4K videos, with potential uses in Virtual Reality (VR) and Augmented Reality (AR) environments.
- The system often requires a high-performance computer, with prospective applications stretching from sports to concerts and filmmaking.
- Commenters highlight impressive progress in dynamic scene rendering using NeRF (Neural Radiance Fields) technology, even sparking speculation on their possible uses in holograms and holographic displays.
