# 2023-10-20

## [ArXiv receives $10M for upgrades](https://news.cornell.edu/stories/2023/10/research-repository-arxiv-receives-10m-upgrades)

- Cornell Tech has procured over $10 million in gifts and grants from the Simons Foundation and National Science Foundation for the support of arXiv, a repository for open-access scholarly articles.
- The funding will facilitate the migration of arXiv to the cloud and the modernization of its code, improving accessibility for researchers. New search and recommendation methods will also be integrated.
- Founded in 1991, arXiv is now run by Cornell Tech and has been a prominent platform for sharing scholarly publications across various fields. The latest funding support will facilitate continued innovation.

### [Reactions](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=37949656)

- ArXiv, the online repository for scientific papers, has received a $10 million funding for upgrades. The hope is to improve services without compromising functionality, with particular emphasis on bettering accessibility and accommodating web-native papers.
- Users expressed concern over reliance on funding from major tech companies, sparking a debate about suitable funding sources, including industry giants or the National Science Foundation.
- Discussions also circled around the cost of living in the Bay Area where ArXiv operates, touching on expenses and perceptions of middle-class income.

## [Early Europeans ate seaweed for thousands of years](https://www.smithsonianmag.com/smart-news/early-europeans-ate-seaweed-and-aquatic-plants-180983102/)

- Archaeologists found evidence that early Europeans consumed seaweed and other aquatic plants, as per an analysis of fossilized dental plaque.
- A study published in Nature Communications analyzed samples from 74 early humans across 28 European sites and found traces of seaweed and aquatic plants in 26 samples.
- These findings challenge the assumption that the advent of farming reduced the consumption of these aquatic foods and present an argument for their nutritional benefits and environmental sustainability.

### [Reactions](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=37943843)

- The text highlights the historical and cultural importance of seaweed as a food source across various cultures, in addition to its usage in cuisine and its health benefits.
- It discusses the decreased consumption of seaweed in Europe due to industrialization, reflecting cultural change tied to progress.
- The text also explores the on-going debate about the benefits and risks of consuming seaweed, including its nutritional content and possible exposure to pollutants.

## [Nota is a language for writing documents, like academic papers and blog posts](https://nota-lang.org/#def-nota)

- Nota is a new language designed for creating documents, such as academic papers and blog posts, that compiles to JavaScript programs, making the documents viewable on any device with a web browser.
- Nota's unique selling point lies in its ability to represent the structure of the content, creating dynamic and accessible documents without the need for extensive web-development knowledge.
- Nota is currently evolving and is open to early adopters who could play a crucial role in shaping the future of document languages.

### [Reactions](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=37950952)

- The article focuses on the development of Nota, a document writing language, as a potential alternative to complex markup languages such as TeX and LaTeX.
- It outlines the challenges inherent in existing tools and emphasizes the need for innovation in document formats, including the inclusion of dynamic elements for interactive experiences.
- The piece also dissects the advantages and disadvantages of PDF and EPUB formats, the hurdles in supplanting LaTeX, and the broader community's varying views on the utility of Nota.

## [Toyota adopts the North American charging standard](https://pressroom.toyota.com/toyota-adopts-the-north-american-charging-standard-to-expand-customer-charging-options/)

- Toyota Motor North America is set to adopt the North American Charging Standard (NACS) for its battery electric vehicles (BEVs) from 2025, based on an agreement with Tesla.
- This decision allows Toyota and Lexus customers to access over 12,000 Tesla Superchargers across North America and is aimed at giving customers more convenient charging options and a wider travel range.
- Certain Toyota and Lexus BEVs will feature NACS ports, and adapters for NACS charging will be provided to customers with vehicles equipped with the Combined Charging System (CCS).

## [Researchers identify largest ever solar storm in 14k-year-old tree rings](https://phys.org/news/2023-10-largest-solar-storm-ancient-year-old.html)

- Researchers have detected the largest known solar storm in ancient tree rings, dated back to 14,300 years ago.
- The study suggests that similar events in the present day could result in dire consequences, such as blackouts, and damage to telecommunications and satellite systems.
- The team stresses on understanding and gearing up for such solar storms to safeguard our global communication and energy infrastructure, revealing there's more to discover about the sun's behavior and related risks.

### [Reactions](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=37944269)

- Tree rings have revealed the largest recorded solar storm, emphasizing the necessity to prepare for future solar storms and shield the electrical grid. The destructive effect of a solar storm on current technology could be devastating.
- There are debates about the effects of solar flares on Earth, with worries about long-term blackouts due to a lack of replacement transformers. Protective measures are not hindered by funding or political will but are focused on the impacts on nuclear plants, water pumps, and goods distribution.
- The potential approaches include grounding breakers during solar storms, forming a Strategic Transformer Reserve, and masking transformers as robots. The logistical and financial costs of maintaining a transformer reserve are weighed against potential outcomes.

## [FCC moves ahead with Title II net neutrality rules in 3-2 party-line vote](https://arstechnica.com/tech-policy/2023/10/fcc-moves-ahead-with-title-ii-net-neutrality-rules-in-3-2-party-line-vote/)

- The FCC has voted 3-2, moving forward with a proposal to revive net neutrality rules, regulating Internet Service Providers (ISPs) as common carriers.
- This plan involves reclassification of broadband as a telecommunications service, enabling the FCC to control ISPs under Title II of the Communications Act, similar to measures taken in 2015 but later repealed.
- The proposal is now open for a public comment period before finalizing, though challenges from the broadband industry in court are anticipated.

### [Reactions](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=37944680)

- The discussion primarily revolves around the effects of various net neutrality laws, with a focal point on California's law and its implications.

- The conversation also explores potential impacts on service plans and ISP monopolies, underlining the necessity for regulations to safeguard consumers along with the contrasting viewpoints on net neutrality, government intervention, and free market.
